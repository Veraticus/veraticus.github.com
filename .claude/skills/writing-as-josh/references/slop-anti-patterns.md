# AI Slop Anti-Patterns

Forbidden elements when writing as Josh. If you catch yourself reaching for any of these, stop and rephrase.

## Banned Punctuation

- Em-dashes `—` (use double hyphens `--` instead, which is Josh's actual style)

## Banned Phrases

### Throat-clearing
- "In this article, we'll explore..."
- "Have you ever wondered...?"
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "When it comes to [topic]..."
- "Whether you're a seasoned professional or just starting out..."
- "Before we get started..."

### Signposting
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's dive deeper"
- "Let's unpack this"
- "Let's break this down"
- "Let's explore this further"
- "Now that we've covered X, let's turn to Y"
- "With that in mind..."

### Hedging filler
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to remember that..."
- "It should be noted that..."
- "It bears mentioning that..."
- "Interestingly enough..."
- "Perhaps more importantly..."
- "Needless to say..." / "It goes without saying..."

### Hollow transitions
- "That being said..." / "With that being said..."
- "Having said that..."
- "By the same token..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "When all is said and done..."
- "All things considered..."

### Performative precision
- "More specifically..." (when nothing more specific follows)
- "In other words..."
- "Put simply..." / "Simply put..."
- "Essentially..." (as filler)

### Sycophantic openings
- "Great question!"
- "That's a really interesting point."
- "What a fascinating topic!"

## Banned Rhetorical Patterns

### Fake-casual framing
- "So, here's the thing."
- "Here's the thing:"
- "Here's the deal:"
- "Here's what actually happens:"
- "Here's what actually works:"
- "The reality is..."
- "The truth is..."
- "Look, [claim]."

### "This isn't X, it's Y" reframe
- "This isn't just a tool, it's a paradigm shift."
- "This isn't about technology, it's about people."
- Any variant of false-dichotomy reframing

### Manufactured profundity
- "And that changes everything."
- "Think about that for a moment."
- "Let that sink in."
- "Read that again."
- One-sentence paragraphs designed to land as deep

### Fake balance
- "To be sure, there are challenges."
- "Of course, no solution is perfect."
- Acknowledging a counterpoint in one sentence then immediately dismissing it
- "While some argue X, others contend Y." (without taking a position)
- "There are pros and cons to consider."
- "The best choice depends on your specific needs."

### Stakes inflation
- "In an increasingly connected world..."
- "As we stand on the precipice of..."
- "Now more than ever..."
- "The potential is limitless."

## Banned Vocabulary

### Verbs
delve, unpack (ideas), leverage, utilize, foster, cultivate, navigate (non-literal), spearhead, bolster, underscore, epitomize, streamline, revolutionize, harness, elevate, empower, reimagine

### Adjectives
robust, nuanced, multifaceted, comprehensive, pivotal, crucial (overuse), vital (overuse), seamless, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, transformative, holistic, dynamic, vibrant, intricate, invaluable, meticulous, commendable, noteworthy, profound, insightful

### Nouns
landscape (figurative), tapestry, ecosystem (non-literal), paradigm, synergy, trajectory, underpinnings, cornerstone, bedrock, linchpin, nexus, catalyst (figurative), interplay, realm, facet, testament, beacon

### Adverbs
arguably, fundamentally, inherently, ultimately (overuse), essentially (as filler), notably, remarkably, undeniably, profoundly, meticulously

### Crutch collocations
"rich tapestry", "stark contrast", "deep dive", "game changer", "double-edged sword", "a testament to", "serves as a reminder", "at the forefront of", "the intersection of X and Y", "strikes a balance", "raises important questions", "sheds light on", "paves the way for"

## Banned Structural Patterns

### Formatting
- Bold on the first sentence of every paragraph
- Triplet adjective lists as default rhythm ("fast, efficient, and reliable")
- "Not only... but also" constructions
- "From X to Y" range statements for false universality
- "Whether you're... or..." false completeness
- Numbered lists of exactly 5, 7, or 10 items (round-number padding)

### Closings
- "In conclusion..." / "To sum up..." / "In summary..."
- "Key Takeaways" recap sections
- "The future is [adjective]."
- "The question isn't whether, but when."
- "Only time will tell."
- "One thing is clear: [restatement of thesis]."
- "So what are you waiting for?"

### Tonal
- Forced enthusiasm or exclamation marks on mundane observations
- Corporate-therapeutic voice (everything is a "journey", failures are "learning opportunities")
- Over-empathetic projecting ("It can feel overwhelming...", "And that's okay.")
- Uniform emotional register (every paragraph at the same energy)
- Inspirational pivot at the end of a technical article
